# W.D. Gann's Trading Methods

## Overview

William Delbert Gann (1878–1955) was an American trader, market forecaster, and author whose methods blended geometry, astronomy, astrology, mathematics, and natural law into a comprehensive market philosophy. He remains one of the most controversial and studied figures in technical analysis history. His core thesis: **markets are not chaotic — they follow universal laws of harmony and repetition**, the same rhythms found in planetary motion, seasonal change, and natural cycles.

Gann's work sits at the intersection of precise mathematics and esoteric knowledge. He drew from the Bible, Freemasonry (some claim he reached the 33rd degree of the Scottish Rite), ancient Egyptian and Greek mathematics, and the scientific work of Michael Faraday on electromagnetic fields. He was dubbed the "Financial Nostradamus."

---

## Biography & Background

- **Born:** June 6, 1878, in Lufkin, Texas
- **Died:** June 18, 1955
- **Background:** Born into a cotton farming family; self-educated in mathematics and market analysis
- **Career:** Began trading commodities and stocks in the early 1900s; reportedly turned modest capital into fortunes through proprietary methods
- **Religion:** Deeply religious; studied the Bible extensively, viewing it as both a spiritual and scientific guide
- **Philosophy:** "Markets move according to natural laws, and price vibrates at specific mathematical levels"

### The Famous 1909 Interview

Gann's most-cited achievement comes from an interview/observation documented around October 1909 (often referenced as appearing in *The Ticker and Investment Digest*, associated with Richard Wyckoff's publication circle). Over approximately 25 trading days, Gann reportedly made **286 trades in cotton and wheat futures, with 264 profitable** — a win rate of ~92.3%. This was allegedly observed and verified by independent witnesses in his office.

Key details:
- Conducted in front of a representative from the publication
- Trades were in both directions (long and short)
- Gann used his proprietary geometric and time-cycle methods
- The demonstration established his reputation as a market forecaster

### Controversy Around His Wealth

Gann's actual trading profits remain debated:
- **Supporters** cite the 1909 demonstration, his multiple real estate holdings, and his ability to fund extensive research travel
- **Skeptics** note that much of Gann's documented income came from **selling courses and books** (which were very expensive for the era — some courses cost $5,000 in 1930s dollars, equivalent to ~$100,000+ today)
- His estate at death was reportedly modest compared to legends of his wealth, though exact figures are disputed
- His son allegedly gave interviews suggesting his father's trading wealth was overstated

The truth likely lies in between: Gann was a skilled trader who also ran a profitable education business. His methods were deliberately kept opaque to preserve their commercial value.

## Core Philosophy: The Law of Vibration

Gann's entire system rests on what he called the **Law of Vibration**:

> "After years of patient study I have proven to my entire satisfaction, as well as demonstrated to others, that vibration explains every possible phase and condition of the market."

Key principles:
1. **Every stock, commodity, or asset vibrates at its own frequency** — determined by its price history, time cycles, and natural mathematical relationships
2. **Time and price are inseparable** — you cannot analyze one without the other
3. **Markets move in cycles** — each cycle is influenced by the previous one, and these cycles can be measured geometrically
4. **The circle (360°) is the master pattern** — all market movements can be mapped to divisions of the circle: 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, 120°, 180°, 270°, 360°
5. **Natural law governs markets** — the same forces that drive planetary orbits and seasonal changes drive market behavior

## The Major Tools & Methods

### 1. Gann Angles (Gann Fan)

The most accessible and widely-used Gann tool. Gann angles express the **geometric relationship between price movement and time**.

#### The 1×1 Angle (45°)
- The most important angle in Gann analysis
- Represents **perfect equilibrium**: one unit of price movement for one unit of time
- When price is **above** the 1×1 line → bullish (market has upward momentum)
- When price is **below** the 1×1 line → bearish (market has downward momentum)
- A break of the 1×1 line signals a significant trend change

#### The Complete Gann Fan

| Angle | Ratio | Meaning | Degree |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|
| 1×8 | 1 price : 8 time | Very weak trend | 7.5° |
| 1×4 | 1 price : 4 time | Weak trend | 15° |
| 1×3 | 1 price : 3 time | Moderate weakness | 18.75° |
| 1×2 | 1 price : 2 time | Below-average strength | 26.25° |
| **1×1** | **1 price : 1 time** | **Perfect balance** | **45°** |
| 2×1 | 2 price : 1 time | Strong trend | 63.75° |
| 3×1 | 3 price : 1 time | Very strong trend | 71.25° |
| 4×1 | 4 price : 1 time | Extremely strong | 75° |
| 8×1 | 8 price : 1 time | Parabolic/blowoff | 82.5° |

#### How to Draw a Gann Fan
1. **Identify the anchor point** — a significant swing high or swing low
2. **Draw the 1×1 line** from that point at a 45° angle (requires proper scaling of your chart)
3. **Add the other angles** radiating from the same point
4. **Interpret**: price bouncing off an angle = support/resistance; price breaking through = moving to the next angle level
5. Both **ascending fans** (from lows) and **descending fans** (from highs) should be drawn

#### Critical Note on Scaling
The biggest challenge with Gann angles is **proper chart scaling**. The 1×1 angle is only truly 45° if the chart's price axis and time axis are scaled so that one unit of price equals one unit of time. Different instruments require different scale factors. Gann was extremely particular about this — he hand-drew his charts at specific scales.

### 2. The Square of 9

The Square of 9 is Gann's most famous and enigmatic tool — a **numerical spiral** that maps price and time onto a geometric framework derived from the circle.

#### Structure
Imagine numbers arranged in an expanding spiral pattern starting from 1 at the center:

```
                    ...
        17  16  15  14  13
        18   5   4   3  12
        19   6   1   2  11
        20   7   8   9  10
        21  22  23  24  25
                    ...
```

- **1** sits at the center
- Numbers spiral outward in a clockwise (or counterclockwise, depending on version) pattern
- Each complete revolution around the center adds one "ring"
- Numbers on the same **radial line** (same angle from center) share a geometric relationship

#### Key Properties
- Numbers along the **cardinal cross** (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°) form a sequence: 1, 2, 4, 7, 13, 21, 31, 43...
- Numbers along the **diagonal cross** (45°, 135°, 225°, 315°) form another sequence: 1, 3, 5, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49... (these are **perfect squares**)
- The relationship between rings follows **square root mathematics**

#### The Square Root Method
To find the next key level from a given price:
1. Take the **square root** of the current price
2. **Add or subtract** 0.25, 0.5, 1.0, or 2.0 (representing 90°, 180°, 360°, or 720° of rotation)
3. **Square the result** to get the next price level

**Example** — Starting price: $100
- √100 = 10.0
- 10.0 + 0.5 = 10.5 → 10.5² = **$110.25** (180° rotation — potential resistance)
- 10.0 + 1.0 = 11.0 → 11.0² = **$121.00** (360° rotation — major resistance)
- 10.0 − 0.5 = 9.5 → 9.5² = **$90.25** (180° rotation down — potential support)
- 10.0 + 2.0 = 12.0 → 12.0² = **$144.00** (720° rotation — strong resistance)

#### Time Application
The same spiral maps **time intervals**. Place a significant date at the center, and the spiral reveals dates where turning points are likely. The intersection of time levels (vertical) and price levels (horizontal) creates **confluence zones** — the highest-probability reversal points.

#### Geometric Selection Method
For timing analysis:
1. Mark significant dates on the grid
2. Overlay geometric shapes (triangles, squares, pentagons, hexagons, etc.)
3. Shapes whose diagonals intersect the maximum number of key dates are preferred
4. Fewer-sided shapes are considered more "geometrically pure" and fundamental

### 3. The Gann Wheel

The Gann Wheel is the **circular representation** of the Square of 9. Instead of a flat spiral grid, imagine the same numbers plotted on a wheel:

- The center represents 0° (or the starting price/date)
- Numbers spiral outward from the center
- Each 360° revolution represents one complete cycle
- The cardinal directions (N, S, E, W = 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°) mark **critical turning points**
- The ordinal directions (NE, SE, SW, NW = 45°, 135°, 225°, 315°) mark **secondary levels**

#### How It Maps Price/Time
- **Price axis**: As you move outward from center, prices increase following the square root spiral
- **Time axis**: The angular position (degrees around the wheel) represents time
- **Confluence**: When a price level and time point align on the same radial line, the wheel suggests a high-probability turning point
- The wheel reveals that prices at certain levels are "harmonically related" — they share the same angular position despite being at different distances from center

#### Practical Use
Traders use the Gann Wheel to:
- Identify **support and resistance levels** that are mathematically related to significant highs/lows
- Calculate **time targets** for potential reversals
- Find **price-time squares** — moments where price and time achieve geometric balance

### 4. The Cosmogram (Cosmic Diagram)

The cosmogram is Gann's most overtly astrological tool — a timing method that **aligns planetary positions with market cycles**.

#### Foundation
Gann's cosmogram is based on the **geocentric zodiac wheel** — a 360° circle divided into 12 signs of 30° each, with planets positioned according to their ecliptic longitude.

#### Planetary Aspects Used
Gann identified key angular relationships between planets:

| Aspect | Degrees | Market Interpretation |
|--------|---------|----------------------|
| **Conjunction** | 0° | Strongest combined effect — major turning points |
| **Sextile** | 60° | Favorable; supportive of existing trends |
| **Square** | 90° | Tension; conflict of energies; potential turning points |
| **Trine** | 120° | Stability; calm; long-term tendencies |
| **Opposition** | 180° | Strong fluctuations; opposing forces |

Additional minor aspects:
- **Semisextile** (30°) — initial phase of a cycle
- **Semisquare** (45°) — first resistance point
- **Sesquisquare** (135°) — late-stage strain, often preceding reversal
- **Quincunx** (150°) — break in harmonic alignment; need for adjustment

#### The Three Rules of the Cosmogram
1. **Rule 1**: When the **Sun and another planet ingress into new zodiac signs on the same day**, it often marks a turning point in market trends. This signals a reset in planetary cycles and a shift in momentum.
2. **Rule 2**: If the ingress occurs over **two consecutive days** rather than one, the likelihood of reversal remains high but may be delayed.
3. **Rule 3**: When **only one body** (Sun or a single planet) ingresses, the signal is weak and requires confirmation from other factors (aspects, lunar phases, eclipses).

#### Gann's Astrological Framework
- Gann viewed the **Sun as the primary energy source** affecting markets through its influence on Earth's electromagnetic field
- He was influenced by **Michael Faraday's** research on solar activity and terrestrial magnetism
- Planetary positions were seen not as direct causes but as **timing signals** — indicators of when collective human psychology would shift
- The cosmogram uses **geocentric** (Earth-centered) positioning, not heliocentric

### 5. The True Eclipse Method

Gann analyzed **solar and lunar eclipses** as precursors to market reversals.

#### Eclipse Classification by Intensity

| Eclipse Type | Signal Strength | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Total Solar** | Strongest | Major reversal signal; watch for trend change within days/weeks |
| **Annular Solar** | Strong | Significant reversal potential |
| **Total Lunar** | Moderate | Notable market shift likely |
| **Partial (Solar/Lunar)** | Weakest | Minor influence; requires confirmation |

#### Key Principle
Eclipses are treated as **warning signals**, not exact turning points. The reversal may occur before, on, or after the eclipse date. Gann recommended using eclipses in combination with other tools (cosmogram, Square of 9) to narrow the timing window.

### 6. Time Cycles

Gann believed **time is the most important factor in market analysis** — more important than price.

#### Key Time Intervals
- **30 calendar days** (1 month / 30°)
- **45 calendar days** (45°)
- **60 calendar days** (60° / sextile)
- **90 calendar days** (1 quarter / 90° / square)
- **120 calendar days** (120° / trine)
- **180 calendar days** (6 months / 180° / opposition)
- **270 calendar days** (270°)
- **360 calendar days** (1 year / full circle)
- **365 calendar days** (solar year)

#### Master Time Cycles
- **1 year** (360° / 365 days)
- **2 years** (720°)
- **5 years** — significant intermediate cycle
- **7 years** — Biblical cycle (Sabbatical)
- **10 years** (decade cycle)
- **20 years** — Jupiter-Saturn conjunction cycle
- **30 years** — Saturn return cycle
- **60 years** — major economic cycle
- **90 years** — grand cycle

#### The 9:5 Ratio
Gann observed that if a price trend runs for 9 time units, it typically corrects for 5 time units — a ratio of approximately 1.618 (the golden ratio / Fibonacci relationship).

### 7. The 50% Retracement Rule

After a strong price move, Gann expected markets to retrace approximately **50% of the move** before continuing. This level represents equilibrium between supply and demand.

- A **hold above 50%** = bullish continuation likely
- A **break below 50%** = potential full reversal
- This was Gann's most basic and accessible rule, predating Fibonacci retracements in popular technical analysis

Additional Gann retracement levels: **25%, 33%, 37.5%, 50%, 62.5%, 67%, 75%** — derived from divisions of the circle (90°, 120°, 135°, 180°, 225°, 240°, 270°).

---

### 8. Squaring Price with Price

One of Gann's lesser-known but powerful techniques:
- Take a significant high or low price
- Calculate the **square root**
- Add/subtract whole numbers or specific fractions
- Square the result to find the next support/resistance

This identifies "vibration levels" where price is likely to stall or reverse, derived purely from the price's own mathematical structure — no time element needed.

**Example**: AOL's low at $7.02 in July 2002 — Gann practitioners noted this was close to a perfect square level (√7.02 ≈ 2.65, and nearby natural squares produce resistance levels at $6.25, $7.56, $9.00, etc.).

## Key Books & Written Works

### Published Books

1. **"Truth of the Stock Tape"** (1923)
   - Gann's first major work on market analysis
   - Focuses on tape reading, market rules, and trading discipline
   - Practical in nature — covers how to interpret price action and volume
   - More accessible than his later, more esoteric works

2. **"The Tunnel Thru the Air, or Looking Back from 1940"** (1927)
   - A **novel**, not a trading manual — but arguably Gann's most studied work
   - Fiction that follows character Robert Gordon, who uses Biblical knowledge and natural law to predict the future
   - Gann himself stated: *"This book contains a valuable secret, clothed in veiled language"*
   - **Alleged predictions embedded in the narrative**:
     - Descriptions of aerial warfare that some interpret as predicting aspects of World War II (written 12 years before the war began)
     - Market cycle predictions encoded in dates and events within the story
     - References to specific price levels and time periods
   - Gann scholars have spent decades trying to decode the "hidden message"
   - The book is as much a statement about Biblical prophecy and natural law as it is about markets

3. **"Wall Street Stock Selector"** (1930)
   - Stock selection and timing techniques
   - Discusses cycle analysis in practical terms

4. **"New Stock Trend Detector"** (1936)
   - Forecasting methods for stock trends
   - Introduces additional timing techniques

5. **"How to Make Profits in Commodities"** (1941/1951)
   - Gann's most practical commodity trading guide
   - Covers cotton, wheat, corn, and other agricultural markets
   - Contains specific trading rules and examples

6. **"45 Years in Wall Street"** (1949)
   - Late-career reflections on market patterns
   - Contains updated rules and observations
   - Discusses the long-term cycles he'd observed over his career

### Courses (Sold Privately at High Prices)

- **"Master Time Factor"** — timing techniques
- **"Master Forecasting Course"** (also called "Master Course") — comprehensive system
- **"Commodities Course"** — specific to commodity markets
- These courses were sold for $3,000–$5,000+ in the 1930s-1950s (equivalent to $50,000–$100,000+ in modern dollars)
- Gann deliberately kept methods obscure in public books, reserving the "real secrets" for paying course students

## Famous Predictions

### Documented Predictions
1. **1909 Cotton Forecast**: Published warning that cotton would not fall below $0.99 by year-end and would jump to $1.45 by spring 1910. The prediction proved remarkably accurate.

2. **1928-1929 Stock Market**: Gann is reported to have warned clients of the impending 1929 crash, though the exact timeline and documentation are debated.

3. **Annual Forecasts**: Gann published annual market forecasts that were reportedly accurate in many years, though complete records are difficult to verify independently.

### The 90% Accuracy Claim
Gann's methods were "reportedly over 90% accurate" according to promotional materials and some biographical accounts. This figure is widely repeated but difficult to independently verify. The 264/286 trades (92.3%) from the 1909 demonstration is the closest thing to a documented verification.

## The Debate: Genius or Charlatan?

### The Case for Gann
- The 1909 documented trading demonstration (if accurate) is extraordinary
- His geometric/mathematical framework is internally consistent and mathematically sophisticated
- Many traders report the Square of 9 and Gann angles produce statistically interesting results
- Time cycle analysis has some overlap with legitimate cyclical research
- His methods predated and anticipated later developments in fractal geometry and market microstructure

### The Case Against Gann
- Most of his documented income came from selling courses and books, not trading
- His estate at death was reportedly modest
- His methods are vague enough to be unfalsifiable — angles can always be redrawn, cycles reinterpreted
- The astrological components have no demonstrated causal mechanism
- Survivorship bias: his successful predictions are remembered; failures are forgotten
- No independent, rigorous audit of his long-term trading performance exists

### The Balanced View
Gann's geometric tools (angles, fans, squares) have genuine utility as support/resistance frameworks — they impose mathematical structure on price data and can identify meaningful levels. His time cycle work captures real seasonal and cyclical patterns. The astrological overlay is the most contentious element: whether planetary positions actually affect markets through electromagnetic or psychological channels remains unproven, but the cyclical framework itself has value regardless of the proposed mechanism.

## Summary: Gann's System in One Paragraph

Gann believed that every market instrument vibrates at a specific frequency determined by the interaction of price and time. Using the 360° circle as his master template, he mapped price movements onto geometric angles (Gann Fan), spiraling numerical relationships (Square of 9), planetary positions (Cosmogram), and eclipse cycles (True Eclipse Method). His system's power comes from finding **confluence** — where multiple tools agree on the same price level and time window for a reversal. The framework treats markets as natural phenomena governed by the same laws as planetary orbits, seasonal cycles, and harmonic vibration. Whether the mechanism is electromagnetic, psychological, or mathematical coincidence, Gann's tools continue to identify meaningful market levels 70+ years after his death.
